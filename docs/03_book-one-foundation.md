# Book I — Story Foundation

Purpose: turn the Bible's premise into a buildable Book I spine, and name the creative decisions that are still open so they get *decided*, not defaulted. Everything here serves canon (Bible v1.0) and the canon tone (hopeful, cinematic, never dystopian).

---

## What the Bible already fixes (the givens)
- **Protagonist:** Dr. Mineral — campus squirrel, secretly founder/Chief Researcher of the Hidden Laboratory; collects discoveries, not treasure.
- **World:** a centuries-old underground research city beneath an ordinary university, run by intelligent animals, unknown to humans.
- **Engine:** an invisible contribution network that records and verifies every contribution — the quiet infrastructure that makes the lab's collaboration possible.
- **Book I must introduce:** Dr. Mineral, the lab, the research philosophy, the animal researchers, the invisible contribution network, and **the first major scientific mystery.**

## Working logline (draft — refine)
*Beneath a sleepy university, a humble squirrel scientist and his lab of animal researchers chase a mystery that the world above doesn't know exists — and the only way to solve it is the one thing the lab was built for: letting many small contributions add up to one large discovery.*

## The shape of Book I (proposed skeleton)
1. **Opening image — the double life.** Dr. Mineral as an ordinary acorn-collecting squirrel topside; a quiet tell drops him into the Hidden Laboratory. Establishes the secret and the wonder in one move.
2. **The world, walked not lectured.** A tour-by-action through a few districts (not all 15) so the reader *feels* the lab. Technology appears as experience, never explanation.
3. **The inciting mystery.** Something is found that shouldn't be — the first major scientific mystery (see open decision A). It can't be solved by one genius; it needs the network.
4. **Contribution as the method.** The plot literally runs on the philosophy: small observations from many researchers, stitched together, move the mystery forward. The invisible network is felt as trust, not described as tech.
5. **A real challenge, cooperatively solved.** Stakes without dystopia. The resolution rewards curiosity and collaboration.
6. **The hold-open ending.** The mystery resolves; a larger question opens — seeding future books without a cliffhanger gimmick.

## Open creative decisions (decide these — don't let them default)
**A. What is the first major scientific mystery?** The Bible leaves this deliberately blank. Three on-canon directions:
- *A signal in the Crystal Vault* — a preserved discovery from the lab's deep past starts behaving in a way no one recorded; a knowledge-lineage mystery.
- *A gap in the Contribution Registry* — a discovery exists with no record of who contributed it; an "every contribution matters" mystery that puts the philosophy itself on stage.
- *Something stirring in Oceanographic / Space / Environmental Systems* — a frontier-science expedition mystery, more adventure-forward.

**B. Who is Dr. Mineral's foil / co-lead?** Book I wants one strong second character. A skeptic? An eager young researcher (lets Dr. Mineral teach, paying off "even he's still a student")? Which species, which department?

**C. How many districts does Book I actually visit?** Recommend 3–4 vivid ones over a shallow tour of 15. Which ones?

**D. Format-first or book-first?** Book I is the canonical anchor everything derives from. But the landing page (Dr. Mineral's first public surface) can ship as a *teaser of canon* before the book is drafted. Decide whether the page leads or follows.

**E. Reading age center of gravity.** Multi-level is canon, but where's the center — middle-grade adventure that adults enjoy, or YA/adult with a layer kids can follow? Affects voice and chapter length.

## First buildable artifacts (once A–E are set)
- One-page synopsis → chapter outline (Save-the-Cat-style beats adapted to the cooperative, non-revenge arc).
- Chapter 1 draft (the double-life opening image).
- Lead-character sheets added to the Canon Registry.
- The landing page repositioned from "Lab Reward Token mascot" to "Dr. Mineral — first public surface of the universe," if E/D point that way.
