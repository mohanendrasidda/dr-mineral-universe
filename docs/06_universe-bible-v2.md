# THE DR. MINERAL UNIVERSE — Universe Bible v2.0 (Living-Universe Expansion)

**Creator:** Mohanendra Siddha · **Status:** CANON. **Deliberately evolves Bible v1.0** (`01_universe-bible-v1.md`). Where v2 and v1 conflict, **v2 wins** — and the change is intentional, flagged here. v1 remains the soul (curiosity, contribution, technology invisible, hopeful tone, no magic). v2 changes the *scope and form*: from a novel-anchored world to a **living universe** that surfaces as an interactive, ever-growing website.

---

## 0. Why v2 exists (the pivot)
v1 produced a beautiful, self-contained **novel**. The product is not a novel. The product is a **world that keeps happening** — explorable, evolving, transmedia. A book ends; this world must not. v2 reorients every future decision toward the universe, with the literary voice kept intact but subordinate to world-building.

> **The test for any new work:** does it make a reader want to *walk into this world and explore it* — and come back next month because it grew? If not, it's serving the plot, not the universe.

## 1. The broadened mission (the single most important change)
**v1 mission:** preserve and honor contribution.
**v2 mission (canon):**

> **"Our laboratory exists to discover, build, protect, preserve, and pass on knowledge that helps future generations contribute more than we could."**

Five verbs, five reasons the civilization exists — **discover · build · protect · preserve · pass on.** This gives the universe room for cybersecurity, blockchain, AI, robotics, distributed systems, education, community, open source, and inventions that don't exist yet. The **Contribution Registry** (the whole focus of Book I) becomes **one department** of a much larger civilization — the *preserve/pass-on* organ, beside the *discover* organs (research wings), the *build* organs (workshops/foundries), and the *protect* organs (security/SOC).

## 2. The laboratory is a co-protagonist (a civilization, not a place)
Treat the laboratory like Hogwarts, Pandora, Coruscant, the Shire — a world people remember more than any single plot. It is:
- **An underground research city** of *thousands* of intelligent-animal researchers, predating modern universities, expanding for centuries — and **still expanding now.**
- Organized into **districts** (the original 15 + many more), each a living quarter with its own work, culture, leaders, machines, sounds, smells, and citizens. Full map: `canon/district-atlas.md`.
- **Alive with motion:** research trains and moving elevators, underground rivers, drones ferrying journals and components, mechanical mining squirrels in glowing crystal tunnels, autonomous lab assistants, weather systems, energy cores, growing crystal forests, living fungus caves, ancient abandoned sectors.
- **A society, not just a workplace:** squirrel neighborhoods, generational family houses, the cafeteria and the bakery, a library district, children learning and elders teaching, music, festivals, holiday traditions, and the annual **Contribution Ceremony.** Full life: `canon/daily-life-and-culture.md`.

## 3. The hidden civilization on the surface — the campus squirrels (original inspiration, reclaimed)
**Every campus squirrel is secretly a member of the laboratory.** This was the founding spark and it is now load-bearing canon. The squirrels you see on any campus — by the library, the engineering building, the fountain — are *agents of the lab*, each with a surface role: one is Head of Archives gathering lost knowledge, one collects discarded components by the engineering building, one watches students, one gathers ideas, one maps weather, one studies architecture, one observes kindness. They all report back. The ordinary campus is the visible tip of an invisible civilization. (See `canon/the-squirrel-network.md`.)

## 4. Many entrances, not one
There is **not** a single secret door. There are **dozens** — tree roots, storm drains, old tunnels, maintenance shafts, hidden elevators, disused observatories, dry fountains — and **each opens onto a different district.** This is canon *and* the perfect website architecture: the homepage is a campus; clicking any entrance descends into a different quarter of the lab. (See `canon/the-squirrel-network.md` → Entrances.)

## 5. A research civilization that *invents* (engineering is on-stage)
The lab doesn't just remember — it **makes.** Show prototypes, failures, debugging, scaffolding, the joy and mess of building. The audience includes students, developers, researchers, and security/blockchain people; give them real engineering culture to fall for. **Blockchain is one invention among many** — it emerges as the lab's answer to specific problems (trustworthy memory, protecting contribution), never as a lecture, never the center. Full tech doctrine + invention tree: `canon/technology-and-engineering.md`.

## 6. The lab grows with versions (the reason to return)
The laboratory **evolves.** Every software release of the website is, in-world, a **physical expansion** — new wings open with cranes and scaffolding, new machines come online, new districts are dug. v1 of the site is a 5-room lab; v8 is 20 rooms; v25 is a whole underground city. The living-content surface is **"TODAY IN THE LAB"** — the world is always doing something. (See `canon/growth-and-versions.md`.)

## 7. Dr. Mineral is allowed to be flawed and funny (Pixar rule)
He was becoming too perfect. Canon now: he forgets to eat, loses himself in experiments, builds twenty things at once, misplaces tools, has terrible handwriting, falls asleep over journals, is afraid of public speaking, is a genuinely terrible dancer — and an amazing engineer who laughs too much and gets giddy over tiny discoveries. **Emotion + humor:** the comedy (a misfired machine, an apprentice causing chaos, a confused robot) makes the emotional beats land harder. (See `canon/characters.md` → flaw sheet.)

## 8. Cinematic set-pieces (for animation + the website)
Build unforgettable visual moments, the kind people remember from films and games:
- The **first descent** should make viewers stop scrolling.
- The **first sight of the central cavern** should be breathtaking.
- The **energy/contribution reactor** pulses like a living heart.
- **Mechanical mining squirrels** move through glowing crystal tunnels.
- **Drones** ferry journals and components overhead.
- The **Hall of Contributors** displays shimmering constellations of past work.
- **Every release physically transforms the lab** — cranes, scaffolding, newly opened wings.
(See `canon/visual-canon.md` → Set-pieces.)

## 9. What carries over unchanged from v1
Curiosity over genius · contribution honored · technology serves people and never lectures · no magic · hopeful/cinematic/educational tone · multi-level storytelling · "our laboratory, never my" · research stewarded not owned · no one ever finished learning. The **firewall + personal-IP** rules (CLAUDE.md) are unchanged and still load-bearing: a fictional SOC/blockchain district is fine; mapping lab lore to any real UNL roadmap is not.
