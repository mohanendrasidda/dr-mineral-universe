# Canon — The Squirrel Network & the Many Entrances

The hidden civilization on the surface, and the many ways down. This is the founding inspiration (the campus squirrels) made load-bearing, and it doubles as the website's homepage architecture. From `../docs/06_universe-bible-v2.md`. Related: [district-atlas](district-atlas.md) · [daily-life-and-culture](daily-life-and-culture.md).

## The core idea
**Every squirrel you see on a campus is secretly a member of the laboratory.** The ordinary, overlooked campus squirrel — the one nobody looks at twice — is the visible tip of an invisible civilization. They live a double life (like Dr. Mineral in Ch 1): an ordinary surface role, and a real role below. They watch, gather, map, and report; the lab learns the surface world through them. This is why a humble campus squirrel can be the founder of a research city: *the whole point is being underestimated.*

## Surface agents (cast as the world grows)
Each agent has a **post** (where they're seen) and a **charge** (what they truly do). A few seeded; add freely.

| Post (where you see them) | Charge (what they really do) | Reports to (district) |
|---|---|---|
| By the **library steps** | Head of Archives — gathers lost & discarded knowledge | Historical Archives / Library District |
| By the **engineering building** | Collects discarded components & broken tech to study/reuse | Engineering Workshops / Robotics |
| On the **quad lawns** | Watches students; learns how humans learn | Educational Academy |
| At the **coffee carts / benches** | Gathers ideas overheard; the lab's idea-scouts | Innovation Gardens |
| On **rooftops & ledges** | Maps weather, light, the seasons above | Environmental Systems / Observatory |
| Around **old buildings** | Studies architecture; finds & tends entrances | The Gatehouses |
| By the **duck pond / commons** | Observes kindness; the lab's heart-keepers | Contribution Registry |
| Near **server rooms / IT** | Watches how the surface world's machines fail & defend | Sentinel Hall (SOC) |

> Rule: surface agents never reveal the lab to humans (canon, unless a major arc changes it). Their cover is *being ordinary.* Humor lives here — near-misses, a dropped acorn-key, a student who almost notices.

## The Many Entrances (canon: there is NOT just one)
There are **dozens** of ways down, and **each opens onto a different district.** This is the website: the homepage is the campus surface; clicking any entrance descends into a different quarter. The entrance and the district it serves are paired.

| Entrance (surface) | Opens onto | Feel |
|---|---|---|
| The **old stone in the oldest building's wall** (Ch 1–2) | Central Research Hall | The classic descent; the breathtaking first view |
| **Tree roots** (the great oak) | Innovation Gardens / Biological Research | Down through living roots into green |
| **Storm drains** | The Conduits (rivers/trains) | Rushing water, echo, transit |
| **Maintenance shafts** | Engineering Workshops / Foundries | Oil, gears, heat |
| **Hidden elevators** (old service lifts) | Central Hall express | A long lift into glowing depth |
| **Disused observatory dome** | Observatory / Space Wing | Up to look down; stars from below |
| **Dry fountains** | The Commons / Neighborhoods | Into daily life, food, warmth |
| **Old tunnels (steam/utility)** | Old Quarters / Abandoned Sectors | The deep, the ruins, the deepest mysteries (🔴) |

> Add entrances as new districts open. Some entrances are public (🟢), some members-only or restricted (🔴) — the same access tiers as the atlas. The acorn-key (Dr. Mineral's) and how access works are explored in `technology-and-engineering.md` and remain partly mysterious (Book I horizon).

## Why this matters for the product
- **Homepage = explorable campus,** not a nav bar. Visitors *find* ways in.
- **Replayability:** different entrances → different first experiences of the world.
- **Reclaims the origin:** the universe literally grows out of the everyday campus squirrel — relatable, charming, and uniquely yours.
