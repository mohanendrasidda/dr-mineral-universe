# Canon — Growth & Versions (the world that keeps happening)

The single most important thing for the website: the lab **evolves.** It is never finished. Every release grows it; visitors return because the world changed. From `../docs/06_universe-bible-v2.md`. Related: [district-atlas](district-atlas.md) · [daily-life-and-culture](daily-life-and-culture.md).

---

## The core rule
**Every software release of the website is, in-world, a physical expansion of the laboratory.** New wings open with cranes and scaffolding; new machines come online; new districts are dug. The changelog is not a list — it's *construction the city celebrates.* This makes growth visible, narrative, and worth returning for.

## The version arc (illustrative)
| Site version | The lab is… | Districts open (example) |
|---|---|---|
| **v1** | A small lab — the first descent + a handful of rooms | Central Hall, Archives, Contribution Registry, Workshop, Academy |
| **v8** | A growing quarter — ~20 rooms, real daily life | + Robotics, Foundries, Hall of Contributors, Commons, Observatory, Sentinel Hall |
| **v25** | An entire underground city | + Neighborhoods, Conduits, Old Quarters, Energy Core, Codeworks, Museums, full festivals |

> Numbers are illustrative — the point is the *shape*: each version visibly enlarges the world. Track real openings in `district-atlas.md`.

## "TODAY IN THE LAB" (the living-content surface)
The world is **always doing something.** Instead of static pages, the site surfaces current life:
- A new wing under construction (scaffolding on the homepage).
- A festival or the annual Contribution Ceremony happening now.
- An experiment in progress; a prototype that just worked (or didn't).
- New contributors' stars kindling in the Hall of Contributors.
- A note on the **Release Board / Town Square**.

This is the antidote to "book ending." The lab never ends; visitors return; the story keeps happening.

## Releases as ceremonies
Each release pairs with a **Version-Launch Celebration** (see culture file): banners, the Bakery, music, the new wing's ribbon. The **Version History Museum** is the walkable archive of every past expansion — the lab remembering how it grew (the mission turned on itself).

## Practical mapping (product ↔ lore)
| Product event | In-world event | Surface |
|---|---|---|
| Ship a feature | Open / reveal a district | New entrance + room |
| Changelog entry | Construction announcement | Release Board |
| Past releases | The lab's own history | Version History Museum |
| New contributor / commit | A star kindles | Hall of Contributors |
| Bugfix | A machine debugged, celebrated | Workshop / Sentinel Hall |
| Roadmap | Wings "planned, not yet dug" (scaffolded outlines) | Town Square plans wall |

## Why this is the moat
A world that visibly grows is a reason to come back that a finished novel can never offer. Growth *is* the product. Every commit makes the universe bigger — literally.
