# Central Research Hall

**Essence:** the beating hub where all galleries meet — the daily market of minds, and the first breathtaking sight of the underground city.

## Who runs it
**Bramble**, a prairie dog, the Hall's *Convener* — not a boss but a connector, the one who keeps a thousand conversations from becoming chaos. Prairie dogs build sprawling social towns, and Bramble runs the Hall like one: she knows everyone's name, everyone's question, and exactly which two strangers ought to be introduced. **Flaw:** she physically cannot resist eavesdropping and matchmaking ideas — she'll abandon her own lunch to drag a confused engineer across the Hall because "you HAVE to meet the otter doing currents, trust me." Key citizens: the bridge-keepers, the criers who call the day's findings, the message-runners.

## Walk in
The Hall hits all your senses at once: gold lamplight pouring up from galleries falling away below, the warm hum you can lean on, the smell of bread drifting from the Commons three levels down, and *noise* — glad, arguing, learning noise. A badger debates an owl about star-drift. A family of mice carries a single enormous diagram between them like a sail. Someone's prototype just worked and a small cheer goes up. By midmorning Bramble has convened four impromptu collaborations and lost her breakfast twice.

## What they make / do
The Hall makes *connections* — its product is the right two minds meeting. It hosts the daily "open question" boards where anyone, from a department lead to a first-day apprentice, posts what they're stuck on; the Hall's gift is that within an hour someone wanders by who can help. Failures are posted as proudly as wins (a tradition borrowed from the Workshop). This is the mission's *discover* and *pass-on* verbs lived as a crowd.

## Secrets & open mysteries
- The Hall's central lamp is the oldest in the lab and no one living knows how it's lit; it has never gone out. Bramble is quietly terrified of the day it might.
- A disused gallery off the Hall's seventh balcony is sealed; the seal is old; the criers don't call its name.

## → Website feature
**The home / atrium** — the site's hub. The breathtaking first view; everything links from here. The "open question" boards become a live community/activity surface; "TODAY IN THE LAB" headlines originate here.
