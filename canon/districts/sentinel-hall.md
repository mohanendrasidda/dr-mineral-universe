# Sentinel Hall — the Watch

**Essence:** the district that *protects* — guarding the lab's knowledge, networks, and the chain from harm, decay, and the occasional curious disaster. The civilization's SOC.

## Who runs it
**Commander Sasha**, a meerkat, head of the Watch. Meerkats post real sentinels who stand tall and sound specific alarm-calls for specific threats — the most natural security culture in the animal world. Sasha is calm, fast, and never off-duty; she runs the Watch on the meerkat principle that *someone is always looking up.* Night shift is held by a parliament of owls (silent, dark-sighted). **Flaw:** Sasha cannot fully relax or delegate the last watch; she's learning, slowly, that a good system means she doesn't have to be the one awake. Comic relief: the rookie sentinels who sound the "everything's fine, just checking" alarm too often.

## Walk in
A calm, focused hall ringed with glowing watch-screens that show the lab's vital signs — the rivers, the Energy Core, the chain, the Gatehouses. Security drones drift on quiet patrol. The mood is *unhurried vigilance*: not paranoia, not dystopia (the lab is hopeful, never grim), but the steady care of people who love a place enough to keep it safe. Mostly, the Watch reports "all quiet — the chain is keeping itself."

## What they make / do
They *protect:* watching for intrusion, decay, and failure; running drills; hardening the chain and the networks; deciding (with the Gatehouses) what's public, member, or restricted. They treat security as care, not war — the goal is that everyone else gets to be curious and open *because* the Watch holds the wall. The *protect* verb, made warm.

## Secrets & open mysteries
- Lately the night owls have logged a faint, regular signal from the abandoned sectors that matches nothing on the maps.
- A restricted incident from "the Long Winter" is sealed in Sasha's records; she'll say only that the lab learned its hardest lesson then.

## → Website feature
**SOC monitoring room** — a live, in-world "is the lab safe?" board (rivers, core, chain, gates all green), with the public/member/restricted access model made literal. Speaks directly to the cybersecurity audience.
