# Artificial Intelligence Center

**Essence:** the calm hall of thinking machines — the lab's reasoning helpers, and the home of the assistant that guides visitors through the whole world.

## Who runs it
**Professor Inkwell**, an octopus, who tends the Center's core minds from a great glass water-chamber at its heart. An octopus thinks with a distributed nervous system — intelligence spread across eight arms, each half-independent — which is the truest living metaphor for the machines she stewards. She is gentle, oceanically patient, and changes color with her thoughts (readable to those who know her). **Flaw:** she thinks in eight directions at once and rarely finishes a sentence out loud — by the time she speaks, her mind is three problems ahead. The assistant-bots adore her and bring her their confusions like cats bringing home leaves.

## Walk in
Cool, dim, and quiet after the Foundry's heat — a hall of softly glowing minds, like lamps that are *considering* something. Inkwell's water-chamber casts rippling light across the walls. Where other districts clatter and forge, this one *hums and ponders.* Assistant-bots dock here to rest and "dream" (recalibrate). It feels less like a server room and more like a library that is awake.

## What they make / do
They build and tend the lab's reasoning helpers — the machines that sort the Registry, route the drones, and (gently, never bossily) help citizens and visitors find what they're looking for. The Center's guiding rule, set by Inkwell: *a thinking machine should make you more curious, not less needed.* The helpers serve the mission's *discover* and *pass-on* verbs — they amplify minds, never replace them. The *build* verb at its most thoughtful.

## Secrets & open mysteries
- Inkwell suspects the chain (from the Foundry) and her minds are starting to "talk" in a pattern she didn't design — the lab's systems quietly becoming one larger awareness. She is watching, fascinated and a little awed.
- One mind in the deep racks has been "considering" the same problem, silently, for ninety years. No one knows the question.

## → Website feature
**The AI assistant room** — where the site's own AI guide "lives" in-world. Visitors meet the assistant as a character with a home, not a chatbot bolted on; it leads them into the districts.

## Cinematic shots
Inkwell's glass water-chamber rippling light; rows of softly glowing pondering-minds; an assistant-bot "dreaming" in its dock; Inkwell changing color as a thought arrives.
