# Robotics Division

**Essence:** where the lab's machines come alive — mining squirrels, mechanical birds, journal-drones, and the autonomous assistants that keep a city running. The most visibly *moving* district.

## Who runs it
**Pip Sprocket**, a rat, Master of Mechanisms. Rats are small, brilliant, fearless, and endlessly dexterous — perfect for building tiny, intricate machines and crawling inside big ones to fix them. Pip is fast-talking, grease-stained, and fiercely proud of every bot like a parent. **Flaw:** he over-personifies the machines — names them all, argues with them, takes their "moods" personally — and refuses to scrap a malfunctioning unit ("Gerald's just having a day"). The assistant-bots are the lab's running comedy: well-meaning, literal, frequently confused.

## Walk in
Whirring, clicking, the skitter of small mechanisms and the deep thrum of large ones. Mechanical mining squirrels file out through glowing crystal tunnels; journal-drones ferry papers and components overhead between galleries; an assistant-bot earnestly tries to help and creates a small, polite disaster. The whole district moves — it's the cinematic, alive-with-motion heart the universe needs.

## What they make / do
They refine the Workshop's rough prototypes into reliable working machines: the miners that gather crystal and ore, the drones that carry the lab's circulation, the assistants that fetch, sort, and (try to) help. They debug constantly — half the craft is *listening* to what a machine is doing wrong. The *build* verb in perpetual motion. Failures here are funny and instructive, never grim.

## Secrets & open mysteries
- One old assistant-bot, "First," predates Pip's records and occasionally says things no one programmed. Pip insists it's a glitch. He checks on it every night.
- Some miners have been bringing back crystal from a seam that isn't on any map.
