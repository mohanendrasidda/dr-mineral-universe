# The Foundries — the Chain Foundry

**Essence:** where the lab *forges* its trust-machines — the engineered home of the chain-of-trust that the Registry invented out of love. Blockchain, shown as craft, never as a lecture.

## Who runs it
**Master Castor**, a beaver, Chief Engineer of the Foundries. Beavers are nature's civil engineers — they build the infrastructure everyone else lives on top of without thinking about it, which is exactly what the chain is. Castor is gruff, precise, allergic to hype, and quietly moved by the thing he tends. **Flaw:** he distrusts anything "clever" — he'll rebuild a working machine three times to make it *simpler*, and he cannot give a short answer about load-bearing tolerances. He and June Ringtail bicker like family (she invents wild, he makes it hold).

## Walk in
Heat and rhythm. A great forge-reactor at the center pulses like a slow heart, throwing molten gold light up the walls; teams move in time to it. Where the Workshop is chaos, the Foundry is *order under pressure* — every link tested, every join proven, because this is the machine the whole civilization's memory rests on. Sparks, the ring of tested metal, the deep satisfaction of things made to last.

## What they make / do
They build the **chain** as physical, dependable infrastructure: each contribution-record forged into a link that holds the one before and is held by the one after, so the whole "guards its own truth" and "keeps itself" — no single keeper, nothing quietly removable. They also forge the lab's other trust-machines (identity-tokens for the Gatehouses, the sync-mechanisms with Quantum Communications). **It is one craft among many** — the Foundry sits beside Robotics and the Energy Core, not above them. Never explained like a whitepaper; you *watch it being made.*

## Secrets & open mysteries
- Castor has noticed the chain doing something he didn't design — beginning to *repair itself* when a link is damaged. He hasn't told anyone. He's not sure he built that.
- The oldest forge-pattern matches a mark from before the founding (Registry tie-in).

## → Website feature
**Live blockchain visualization** — looking into the Chain Foundry at work: links forging, the chain glowing and self-checking. The dev/blockchain audience sees the real systems the lab builds, framed as a place, not a dashboard.

## Cinematic shots
The forge-reactor pulsing like a living heart; a single link cooling from gold to dark; Castor testing a join by ear; the chain visibly re-knitting a damaged link.
