# The Codeworks — Loom Hall

**Essence:** where the lab "weaves" its software and systems — the in-world home of code, repositories, and the living tapestry of everything the lab has built. The GitHub room.

## Who runs it
**Mistress Aran**, a spider, Master Weaver of the Codeworks. A spider builds structures of pure thread — webs that are networks, traps, and homes at once — which is what code is: logic woven into something that holds weight. Aran sees every system as a tapestry; she can feel a flaw three threads away by the change in tension. **Flaw:** she cannot leave a finished weave alone — she'll unpick a working tapestry at midnight because she found "a cleaner thread," and she speaks entirely in weaving metaphor, so half the lab nods politely without understanding a word.

## Walk in
A tall hall of great looms, each one threading glowing logic into bright moving fabric — commits landing as new woven rows, branches splitting and rejoining as separate weaves, the whole history of a system hanging as a tapestry you can read top to bottom. Threads of light run between looms where systems depend on each other. It's mesmerizing: code made visible, beautiful, and *structural.*

## What they make / do
They weave and maintain the lab's software — the patterns behind the Foundry's chain, the AI Center's minds, the Conduits' routing. Every project is a loom; every contributor adds rows; the tapestry remembers who wove what (threaded straight into the Contribution Registry). They review each other's weaves for weak threads (the lab's version of code review) — kindly, because a dropped stitch anywhere can run the whole cloth. The *build* verb, made elegant.

## Secrets & open mysteries
- One ancient loom in the corner still weaves on its own, slowly, working a pattern no living weaver started or understands.
- Aran has found her tapestries beginning to *self-repair* dropped threads — the same thing Castor saw in the chain and Inkwell saw in the minds. Three districts, one quiet phenomenon. None of them have compared notes yet.

## → Website feature
**The GitHub room** — repositories as living looms; commits as woven rows; the project history as a tapestry you walk. Makes open-source and the lab's real code feel like a place the dev audience can explore.

Great looms threading glowing logic; a branch splitting into a separate weave and rejoining; light-threads of dependency between looms; the self-weaving ancient loom in the corner.
