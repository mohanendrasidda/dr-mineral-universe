# Historical Archives

**Essence:** the lab's deep memory of *what it was like* — not records that a thing was done, but the doubts, cold winters, and courage behind it. Where the founding still breathes.

## Who runs it
**Maddox**, a pack rat, Head of Archives — and the surface agent you'd see lurking by the library steps, gathering lost and discarded knowledge. Pack rats build *middens* that really do preserve materials for tens of thousands of years; Maddox's whole being is "keep it, it'll matter." **Flaw:** he cannot throw *anything* away — his office is an avalanche of "important" scraps, and finding a specific journal can take him a delighted, maddening hour. He narrates his own searches aloud.

## Walk in
Warm, dim, close — a honeycomb of reading-cells worn into stone, shelves sagging with the patient weight of years, the dry-sweet smell of a great deal of old paper over something like the inside of an ancient tree. Floating archive-lifts drift overhead carrying journals to readers. It's the opposite of the ticking Registry: the Archives are for *understanding,* not honoring. Most creatures would rather be honored than understood, so Maddox is often gloriously alone with the founders.

## What they make / do
They preserve and *interpret* — cross-reading old journals to recover lost context (as in Book I: the founding age, "the keeping," Old Hazel dissolving into the multitude). They teach the lab to *hear its own past correctly* — that "too many to name" was a bow, not a blank. The *preserve* verb as scholarship.

## Secrets & open mysteries
- The illuminated Old Hazel pages — beautiful, beloved, and a partial truth the Archives once mistook for the whole.
- Maddox keeps one cell locked: journals from the abandoned sectors that "aren't ready to be read yet."

## → Website feature
**Documentation library / research journals** — the readable, browsable knowledge surface (sister to the Codeworks docs). Long-form lore, dev-docs, and "how the lab thinks" all live here as explorable stacks.
