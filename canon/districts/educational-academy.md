# Educational Academy

**Essence:** where the young are taught and the old keep learning — the *pass-on* verb made into classrooms, and the lab's promise that no one is ever finished.

## Who runs it
**Madam Oona**, an elephant — the Academy is the one hall in the lab built tall enough for her, which is exactly why it was built that way: the founders wanted the teacher who never forgets and never rushes. Elephants remember across lifetimes, mourn, teach their young for years; Oona embodies the lab's longest patience. **Flaw:** she answers no question directly — she tells a wandering story and trusts you to find the answer inside it, which delights the patient and maddens the hurried. (Dr. Mineral is enrolled in her dancing class. He is, by her own gentle assessment, "a gifted scientist.")

## Walk in
Warm classrooms carved in tiers of stone, full of light and chalk-dust and the particular happy noise of minds clicking on. Children of a dozen species learn beside grey-muzzled department heads brushing up on a new craft — because here, status means nothing and curiosity means everything. Apprenticeship pairings (like Tally and Vellum) begin here. The youngest hall and the oldest value, in one room.

## What they make / do
They make *the next generation able to contribute more than this one* — the literal text of the mission. Teaching is hands-on: you learn the Registry by walking its rows, the Foundry by forging a link, kindness by sitting with a frightened thing. They also run **onboarding** for newcomers — the first, gentle descent into the world. The *pass-on* verb, daily.

## Secrets & open mysteries
- Oona remembers lessons taught by teachers no record names — she may be a living bridge to the founding age the Archives can't reach.
- The Academy's oldest classroom has a lesson carved into its wall in a script no current teacher can read.

## → Website feature
