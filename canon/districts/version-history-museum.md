# Version History Museum

**Essence:** a walkable timeline of how the laboratory itself grew, era by era — the changelog as a place you can stroll through. The lab remembering its own becoming.

## Who runs it
**Curator Burrows**, the old mole from Book I — once the researcher who sat with still paws, afraid his sixty years of small work would be forgotten; now the beloved keeper of the lab's own story of growth. (A deliberate arc payoff: the one who feared being forgotten became the one who makes sure nothing is.) He is gentle, proud, and inexhaustibly nostalgic. **Flaw:** ask him one question about any past expansion and you lose an afternoon — he relives every era in loving, exhaustive, tangent-filled detail, and he will not be hurried.

## Walk in
Each hall of the museum is a past era of the lab, preserved as it was: the five-room founding lab; the first great dig; the winter the Energy Core came online; the season the chain was forged. Cranes and scaffolding hang frozen mid-expansion, so you walk *through* the construction of the world. Burrows shuffles between exhibits turning lamps up as you near, like the stair in Chapter 2, narrating.

## What they make / do
They preserve the *story of growth itself* — every wing opened, every district dug, every invention's first day. It's the mission's *preserve* and *pass-on* verbs aimed at the lab's own history, so citizens (and visitors) can see that the world was always becoming, and still is. Every new release adds a new hall; the museum is never finished, by design.

## Secrets & open mysteries
- The earliest hall is incomplete — the museum's record of the founding has the same gaps the Registry had, and Burrows is quietly, doggedly filling them.
- One sealed hall is dated to "the Long Winter" (the restricted incident in Sentinel Hall's records). Burrows won't open it. Yet.

## → Website feature
**Version history / changelog as a walkable museum** — releases as halls you stroll, complete with the construction. Turns a dry changelog into an experience that rewards returning visitors with "look how far the lab has come."

## Cinematic shots
Halls of frozen cranes and scaffolding; the tiny five-room founding lab beside the vast present city; Curator Burrows turning up lamps as he narrates; the sealed Long-Winter hall.
