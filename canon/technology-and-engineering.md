# Canon — Technology & Engineering

The lab is a research civilization that *invents.* This file gives the engineering culture and the invention tree, and fixes how blockchain fits: one craft among many, emerging naturally. For the dev/researcher/security/blockchain audience, this is where the world earns their love. From `../docs/06_universe-bible-v2.md`. Related: [district-atlas](district-atlas.md) · [lore-ledger](lore-ledger.md).

## Engineering is on-stage now (show the making)
v1 hid the technology. v2 still never *lectures* it — but it **shows the making**: prototypes, failures, debugging, scaffolding, the joy and mess of building. Canon texture to use:
- **Prototypes & failures** — half-built machines, things that don't work yet, the **Prototype Graveyard** in the Old Quarters where noble failures rest honored (failure is data, never shame — see Book I Ch 10).
- **Debugging** — the lab "listens" to misbehaving machines the way Dr. Mineral measured an acorn; finding the fault is a beloved craft, often funny.
- **The joy of making** — building is celebrated; a working prototype gets a small festival.
- **No magic** — everything is engineered. When it looks magical (motion-woken lamps, the acorn-key, the self-keeping chain), there is a real mechanism, revealed sparingly and later.

## The invention tree (blockchain is ONE branch)
The lab invents across many fields, each answering a real problem of the mission (discover · build · protect · preserve · pass-on):

| Field | In-world form | Mission verb | Lives in |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Materials / energy** | Crystal reactors, the Energy Core, advanced materials | build / protect | Mineral Sciences, Energy Core |
| **Robotics** | Mechanical mining squirrels, mechanical birds, drones, autonomous lab assistants | build | Robotics Division |
| **Artificial intelligence** | Reasoning helpers; the lab's thinking assistants | build | AI Center |
| **Distributed systems & networks** | Quantum communication; how the city stays in sync | build / protect | Quantum Communications, The Conduits |
| **Security (cyber)** | The Watch / Sentinel Hall (SOC); security drones; protecting knowledge & the chain | **protect** | Sentinel Hall |
| **Trustworthy memory (the chain)** | The chain-of-trust remembering, forged in the **Chain Foundry** | **preserve** | Foundries, Contribution Registry, Memory Chambers |
| **Software / systems craft** | The Codeworks / Loom Hall — "weaving" the lab's systems | build | Codeworks |
| **Bio / environment** | Living air, crystal forests, fungus caves, weather systems | discover / protect | Biological Research, Environmental Systems |

## How blockchain fits (the careful part)
- **It emerges, it doesn't dominate.** Book I shows its *birth as an idea* (Tally's chain of trust, born of love — never named as tech). Later, the lab *builds* it in the **Chain Foundry** as the engineered answer to a real need: a memory that keeps itself, that no single keeper holds, that guards its own truth, so contribution can be protected and passed on faithfully across generations.
- **One of many.** It sits beside AI, robotics, security, energy — never the center. A visitor should meet it the way they meet the reactor or the drones: as one wonder in a working civilization.
- **For the audience:** the website can let visitors *explore* the systems the lab is building (the **Live blockchain visualization** = watching the Chain Foundry work; the **SOC monitoring room** = looking into Sentinel Hall). The lore makes each technical feature feel like a real place, not a dashboard bolted on.
- **Still never a whitepaper.** If a scene/page explains the chain like a spec, it's broken. Lead with the place, the people, the problem being solved.
