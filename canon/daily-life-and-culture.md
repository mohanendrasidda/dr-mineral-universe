# Canon — Daily Life & Culture

The laboratory is a society, not a workplace. This is what makes it unforgettable and what fills "TODAY IN THE LAB" with life. From `../docs/06_universe-bible-v2.md`. Related: [district-atlas](district-atlas.md) · [growth-and-versions](growth-and-versions.md).

---

## Who lives here
Thousands of intelligent animals of many species, in families and generations. Most are not famous researchers — they are bakers, train-drivers, gardeners, teachers, gatehouse-keepers, children. The lab works *because* of them, not despite them; this is the mission lived ("every contribution matters"). Citizens have homes, not just stations.

## Where life happens
- **The Neighborhoods** — squirrel quarters and **generational family houses** carved into the warm cliffs of the deep galleries; warm windows, washing strung between roots, doors that have known the same families for centuries.
- **The Commons & the Bakery** — the cafeteria where the whole city eats and gossips; the bakery whose smell reaches three galleries up and is, quietly, how newcomers first feel at home. (Dr. Mineral burns his own toast every morning; the bakery is a running joke and a comfort.)
- **The Library District** — public reading, study, the gentle daytime hum of a city that loves to learn.
- **The Conduits** — research trains and moving elevators citizens ride to work; the city's circulation, full of small daily-life moments.

## Education across generations (the *pass-on* verb, lived)
- **Elders teaching, children learning** is the most sacred ordinary thing in the lab. The Educational Academy and the Library District are full of it.
- No one is ever "finished" — even department leads attend lessons; even Dr. Mineral is enrolled in something he's bad at (currently: dancing).
- Apprenticeship (like Tally's) is how knowledge and trust pass hand to hand — the human-scale version of the chain.

## Festivals, traditions, ceremonies (the calendar that keeps the world happening)
| Event | What it is | Website tie-in |
|---|---|---|
| **The Annual Contribution Ceremony** | The civilization's high holiday: the year's contributions are honored in the Hall of Contributors; new stars are kindled. Joyful, tearful, communal | A real yearly site moment; the Hall lights up |
| **Version-Launch Celebrations** | Whenever a new wing/invention opens, the city throws a festival — banners, music, the Release Board, food from the Bakery | Maps to each site release ("the lab grew today") |
| **The Founding Remembrance** | Quiet day honoring "the keeping" and the nameless keepers (Book I) | Story/museum feature |
| **Lantern nights, harvests, name-days** | Smaller seasonal traditions that make the world feel lived-in | "TODAY IN THE LAB" flavor |

## Music, sound, and texture
The lab has its own music (work-songs, train-songs, the bakery's morning whistle), its own sounds (the hum, the ticking Registry, the river, the chiming clock towers), its own smells (bread, oil, wet stone, green growing things, old paper). These textures are canon — use them to make any scene or page feel *real and present*.

## Humor & warmth (the Pixar register)
Everyday comedy is canon and welcome: apprentices causing chaos, a confused assistant-bot, a machine that misfires, the eternal toast joke, a famous researcher who can't find their own tools. The funny beats are not decoration — they earn the emotional ones. Keep it kind; never cynical.

## What this gives the universe
A bottomless well of *small true scenes* — exactly what a living website, animations, and future books need to keep happening. The plot is never the only thing going on; the city is always alive in the background.
