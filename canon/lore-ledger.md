# Canon — Lore Ledger

Living continuity file: **facts future stories must not contradict.** Add new locked facts as the world grows; flag any deliberate canon change in the commit message.
Seeded from `docs/02_canon-registry.md` (Universe Bible v1.0). Related: [characters](characters.md) · [departments](departments.md) · [visual-canon](visual-canon.md).

## Locked facts
1. The Hidden Laboratory predates modern universities and has expanded for centuries.
2. It is unknown to humans (unless a major arc deliberately changes that).
3. No magic — every phenomenon has a scientific explanation.
4. Contribution is preserved/verified by invisible infrastructure; characters rarely name the technology.
5. Research is stewarded, not owned.
6. No character is ever "finished" learning — even Dr. Mineral remains a student.
7. **The laboratory's central mission (v2 — broadened; supersedes the v1 contribution-only framing):** *"Our laboratory exists to discover, build, protect, preserve, and pass on knowledge that helps future generations contribute more than we could."* The **Contribution Registry is one department** (the preserve/pass-on organ) of a larger research civilization that also discovers, builds, and protects. Blockchain is **one invention among many,** not the mission. Plant via environment/action; never lecture it. (See `../docs/06_universe-bible-v2.md`.)
8. **Dr. Mineral never says "my laboratory" — always "our laboratory."** He does not claim it; it belongs to many, across generations.
9. The way into the lab is **ingenious forgotten engineering, not magic.** The single acorn he carries is a key that *responds* — never random — explained (engineered) only much later.

## Tone & philosophy locks (from the Bible)
- Hopeful, optimistic, cinematic, educational, emotionally grounded — never cynical or dystopian.
- Multi-level: adventure for younger readers, scientific/philosophical depth for older — same text.
- Celebrates contribution over wealth, discovery over competition, collaboration over ownership.
- **Blockchain is invisible.** Characters experience its benefits (preserved contribution, verified discovery, reputation, lineage); they rarely name it. If a draft explains the chain, it's broken.
