# Book I — Chapter Outline (proposal)

Save-the-Cat-style beats **adapted to the cooperative, non-revenge arc** (no villain, no defeat — the antagonist is a gap in trust, resolved by curiosity + collaboration). ~16 short middle-grade chapters across the Bible's 6-beat Book I skeleton. Each chapter notes the beat it serves and the canon it must honor.

> Legend — beat from `../docs/03_book-one-foundation.md`: ① opening image/double life · ② world walked not lectured · ③ inciting mystery · ④ contribution as method · ⑤ real challenge cooperatively solved · ⑥ hold-open ending.

## Act I — The double life and the impossible gap (Ch 1–5)
1. **Acorns, Above.** Dr. Mineral as an ordinary campus squirrel; warmth, humor, a hint of an unusual spark. *(① — establish topside)*
2. **The Tell.** A quiet, science-not-magic tell drops him into the Hidden Laboratory. First wonder. *(① → ②)*
3. **The Lab, Walked.** Tour-by-action through **Central Research Hall** — technology felt as experience, never explained. *(②)*
4. **The New Apprentice.** Meet the young magpie, assigned to the **Contribution Registry**; establish "every contribution matters" as lived practice, not a speech. *(② — plants the philosophy)*
5. **The Unsigned Discovery.** In the **Crystal Vault**, the apprentice spots a real discovery with *no contributor on record*. The impossible gap. *(③ — inciting)*

## Act II — Asking, not solving alone (Ch 6–11)
6. **The Easy Answers, Refused.** Dr. Mineral declines to claim it or bury it; chooses to *ask*. Establishes the method and his character. *(③ → ④)*
7. **Why It Matters.** Stakes without dystopia: if one contribution can vanish, trust itself frays. Felt, not lectured. *(④)*
8. **First Fragments — the Registry.** Gathering small observations inside the Contribution Registry; the network felt as memory and trust. *(④)*
9. **The Deep Past — Historical Archives.** Travel to the **Historical Archives**; the discovery's roots reach further back than expected. *(② + ④ — second district, lineage)*
10. **A Wrong Turn That Isn't.** A dead-end observation reframes the question — failure as data, curiosity rewarded. *(④ — the "still a student" payoff begins)*
11. **The Pattern.** Fragments start to add up; the pair realize no *single* name was ever the answer. Midpoint turn. *(④ → ⑤)*

## Act III — Repairing the lab, not defeating a foe (Ch 12–16)
12. **Many Hands.** The truth: the discovery was built by many, over a long time — too distributed for the old record to hold. *(⑤)*
13. **The Hard Part.** The real challenge: how do you *honor* a contribution that belongs to everyone? Cooperative problem, not a fight. *(⑤)*
14. **Fresh Eyes.** The apprentice's brand-new observation unlocks the fix — the smallest, newest contribution matters most. *(⑤ — theme lands)*
15. **Restoring the Lineage.** They repair the Registry itself and honor the many; resolution rewards curiosity and collaboration, no one defeated. *(⑤)*
16. **The Thread.** The restored record reveals one faint thread leading deeper — an older question opens. Hold-open, not cliffhanger. *(⑥)*

## Canon guardrails for drafting
- Technology stays **invisible** — if a chapter explains the contribution network, rewrite it as something *felt*.
- Tone never tips cynical/dystopian; challenges are real but solvable through cooperation.
- Multi-level: adventure on the surface, philosophical depth underneath, **same text**.
- Consult `../canon/` before adding any character/district; update it after. Cast openings (apprentice's name, Registry Keeper) as the draft demands and log them.
