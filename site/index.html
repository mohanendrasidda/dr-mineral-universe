01 — The pilot

Built in the open,
taught across three tracks.

This is a cybersecurity reward research project, not a coin launch. A small team inside the SOC designs the system, builds it, and writes the security reviews, while picking up the craft together along the way.

track 01

Blockchain & Solidity

The hands-on build. We work through Solidity, the EVM, and what it takes to ship a real dApp, learning the craft as a team rather than from a slide deck.

track 02

Engineering discipline

How real teams work: a clean repo, clear contribution norms, a sprint cadence, and everyone's name on what they touched.

track 03

Tokenomics research

The design challenge. The team works out the reward system's economics themselves, which is the part that makes it theirs.

02 — The reward system

The Lab Reward Token

The cohort builds one thing end to end: a small but working reward system. It's the teaching vehicle and the portfolio piece. By summer's end it's a clean repo with named commits, security writeups, and a CI pipeline.

STEP 01

Contribute

Someone does the work: ships secure code, finds a bug, or finishes a security task. It's tied to their name.

STEP 02

Earn

A token is issued for verified work. We design issuance carefully so it can't be quietly gamed.

STEP 03

Govern

Token holders vote on a few decisions. The cohort designs the voting so it stays honest.

STEP 04

Redeem

Tokens redeem for something inside the system. This path gets the most care in the design.

↳ This is a sandbox and a pilot.  The mechanics here stay generic on purpose. The specific campus version, the numbers, and any rollout are still being de-risked and go through the right channels, so they aren't on this page.
03 — The program

An eight-session arc

Each session builds on the last and ends pointing at the next. Here's the planned shape of the cohort.

01
Session 01

Foundations & threat framing

What blockchains guarantee, what they don't, and how a security background shapes the way we build.

02
Session 02

Web2 vs Web3 & the 2026 toolkit

The groundwork: how Web3 differs from Web2, what the current toolchain looks like, and a first pass at token mechanics.

03
Session 03

Solidity fundamentals

The language, how the EVM runs it, and the security pitfalls worth designing around from the start.

04
Session 04

ERC-20 construction

Build the token core, write the tests, deploy to a local chain. Live, in the room.

05
Session 05

Reward & distribution logic

How tokens get earned and moved, and how to keep that fair and hard to game.

06
Session 06

Governance & redemption

Voting and the redemption path, and how to design both so they hold up.

07
Session 07

Peer security reviews

The team reviews each other's contracts against a shared checklist and logs every finding.

08
Session 08 · Capstone

Capstone presentations

Everyone presents what they built and secured. That writeup is the thing they keep.

04 — The design challenge

The team owns the design

Nobody hands the cohort a finished blueprint. They work out the reward system's rules themselves, which is what gets them invested. These are the questions they're chewing on.

Q—01

Trust & verification

What in a reward system has to be trustless, and what just needs to be checkable after the fact?

Q—02

Fair participation

How do you keep rewards honest when fake participation is cheap to manufacture?

Q—03

On-chain vs off

Which parts actually belong on a ledger, and which are better kept off-chain?

Q—04

Easy to review

How do you design something a teammate can audit without much friction?
